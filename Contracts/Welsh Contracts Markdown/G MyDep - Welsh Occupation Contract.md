**EXPLANATORY INFORMATION AND DEFINITIONS**

Welcome to the Welsh Fixed Term Standard Contract under the Renting Homes (Wales) Act 2016 (“the Act”). The Contract-Holder should keep this document in a safe place, because it governs the relationship between the Contract-Holder and the Landlord of the Contract-Holder’s home, as well as any letting or managing agent acting for the Landlord, for the entire period the Contract-Holder lives in the property. It also sets out both parties’ rights and obligations. The Contract-Holder should read it carefully and raise any questions with the Landlord or the Landlord’s agent.

Because this is a Fixed Term Standard Contract, it runs for a set period. If the Contract-Holder stays in the property after the fixed term ends, the Contract will automatically convert into a Periodic Standard Contract. This means it continues on the same terms as the original contract until either party serves notice to end it. During a Periodic Standard Contract, certain terms may be varied by serving notice. Some clauses apply only to periodic contracts; these are listed in Annex 3\.

The Landlord must give the Contract-Holder a written statement of terms that explains the Contract-Holder’s rights and duties under the Act, together with any other terms agreed between the Landlord and the Contract-Holder. This Agreement is that written statement. The Contract-Holder has the right to receive a copy of the written statement within 14 days of the occupation date, although it may be provided earlier. The statement must be supplied free of charge. If the Contract-Holder requests extra copies, the Landlord may charge for providing them.

If, before the Act came into force, the Contract-Holder had an Assured Shorthold Tenancy for the premises, the Contract-Holder will hold a “Converted Contract”. Where there is a Converted Contract, the Contract-Holder is entitled to receive the written statement within six months from 1 December 2022\. The statement may be sent electronically if the Contract-Holder has supplied an email address for contact.

If the Contract-Holder does not receive the written statement within the timescales above, the Landlord may have to pay compensation for each day after the occupation date that the statement remains outstanding. Compensation is equivalent to a day’s rent, up to a maximum of two months’ rent. If the Landlord deliberately failed to provide the statement, the Contract-Holder may apply to the court for higher compensation.

The Contract-Holder has the right to seek independent advice about this Contract or dispute resolution from, for example: the Contract-Holder’s solicitor; the Welsh Government website; or another advice body such as Shelter Cymru or the Citizens Advice Bureau.

Any disputes arising from this Contract may be decided in the County Court. However, if the Contract-Holder experiences an issue with the premises, they should first contact the Landlord or the Landlord’s agent to try to resolve it.

Some rights under the Act cannot be changed, although certain rights require the Landlord’s consent. In most cases, that consent cannot be unreasonably withheld. Any term needing consent is identified within the Contract.

The Contract-Holder may be held responsible for anti-social behaviour occurring in the premises even where the Contract-Holder did not personally carry it out. Anti-social behaviour includes excessive noise, verbal abuse, physical assault, and domestic abuse (including physical, sexual, psychological, emotional, or financial abuse).

The Contract-Holder must not allow the premises to become overcrowded by permitting more people to live there than the maximum number allowed. Part 10 of the Housing Act 1985 sets out the basis for calculating the “maximum number allowed”.

The Contract-Holder cannot be evicted unless the County Court serving the area where the premises are located makes a Possession Order, except where the Contract-Holder abandons the premises. Before granting a Possession Order, the Court must be satisfied that the Landlord has followed the proper process and that one of the situations below applies. Where a notice can be served only once the Contract is periodic, that situation is shown in italics.

1. The Contract-Holder has breached one or more terms of this Contract (including non-payment of rent, engaging in or threatening anti-social behaviour or other prohibited conduct, or failing to take proper care of the premises), and it is reasonable to evict the Contract-Holder.

2. The Contract-Holder is in serious rent arrears (for example, where rent is payable monthly and at least two months’ rent is unpaid).

3. The Landlord is able to provide suitable alternative accommodation, an “Estate Management” ground under Section 160 of the Act applies, and it is reasonable to evict the Contract-Holder.

4. Where the Contract is within Schedule 9B of the Act, the Contract-Holder has been given at least two months’ notice to leave under Section 186 of the Act.

5. Where the Contract includes Section 173 of the Act (Landlord’s notice) as a term, the Landlord has served notice under Section 173 requiring the Contract-Holder to give up possession on the date stated in the notice, and the Landlord must also show that:

    a. No restrictions on serving a Section 173 notice apply. This includes restrictions in Section 75 (restriction on terminating tenancies for unlicensed HMOs) and Section 98 (restriction on terminating tenancies for unlicensed houses) of the Housing Act 2004(1), and Section 44 (restriction on terminating tenancies) of the Housing (Wales) Act 2014(2).

    b. The Contract-Holder was given at least six months’ notice, and the notice was not served within the first six months after the occupation date, unless the Contract falls within Schedule 8A(3) or Schedule 9(4) of the Act.

    c. Where the Contract falls within Schedule 8A of the Act, the Contract-Holder was given at least two months’ notice.

6. Where the Contract includes Section 194 of the Act (Landlord’s break clause), the Landlord has served notice under Section 194 requiring the Contract-Holder to leave, and:

    a. No restrictions on serving a Section 194 notice apply.

    b. The Contract-Holder is given at least six months’ notice, the notice is served at least 18 months after the occupation date, and the fixed term was at least two years — except where the Contract falls within Schedules 8A, 9, or 9C of the Act.

    c. Where the Contract falls within Schedule 8A of the Act, the Contract-Holder was given at least two months’ notice.

    d. Where the Contract falls within Schedules 8A and/or 9 and/or 9C of the Act, the Contract-Holder was given the relevant notice required to end that particular type of Contract, and the notice served is the correct notice for that Contract type.

For the avoidance of doubt, it is unlikely that this Contract falls within Schedules 8A, 9, 9B, or 9C of the Act.

Although only the Contract-Holder is named in this Contract, any other person living with the Contract-Holder in the premises may acquire a right to succeed to the Contract if the Contract-Holder dies during the term.

In addition to signing this Contract, the Contract-Holder may be asked to sign a check-in report or inventory listing the Landlord’s fixtures, fittings, and any other items provided for the Contract-Holder’s use. The Contract-Holder will also receive the following documents relating to the premises, and acknowledges receipt on the final page of this Contract:

* The Energy Performance Certificate (EPC).

* A current Gas Safety Certificate, if the property has a gas supply.

* Details of the tenancy deposit protection scheme used, including how the Contract-Holder can recover the deposit at the end of the Contract.

* A checklist of key deposit registration information generally referred to as “required information” (part of which is in Annex 4).

* The Electrical Installation Condition Report (EICR).

If any of these documents are missing when the Contract-Holder signs this Contract, the Contract-Holder should speak to the letting agent, as these documents are as important as the Contract itself.

This Contract contains four categories of clauses: Key Terms, Fundamental Terms, Supplementary Terms, and Additional Terms.

Key Matters are the core elements of the Contract and are shown on page 6\.

Fundamental Terms are mandatory terms required by the Act. Some Fundamental Terms can be changed, but only where the change improves the Contract-Holder’s position. Section 33 of the Act also permits minor editorial amendments provided the meaning of the Fundamental Term is not altered. Fundamental Terms that cannot be changed are marked with (F). Fundamental Terms that can be changed (only to improve the Contract-Holder’s position) are marked with (F+).

Once the Contract-Holder has been given this Contract, the rules above on Fundamental Terms apply.

Supplementary Terms are clauses set out in regulations made by the Welsh Ministers from time to time and are automatically included in Standard Contracts. Supplementary Terms may be altered or removed for the benefit of either party. Any Supplementary Term in this Contract is marked with (S). If the Contract is a Converted Contract, Supplementary Terms do not automatically apply.

Where a Fundamental Term or Supplementary Term has been altered or removed, the deleted wording is shown struck through, and any inserted wording is shown in italics, so changes are clear.

Additional Terms are negotiated between the parties and include all clauses that are not Key Matters, Fundamental Terms, or Supplementary Terms. Additional Terms must not conflict with any Key Matter, Fundamental Term, or Supplementary Term. If this is a Converted Contract, the existing terms of the earlier contract become Additional Terms and continue to apply unless they conflict with a Key Matter or Fundamental Term. If an Additional Term is unfair under Section 62 of the Consumer Rights Act 2015, it will not be binding.

# Definitions

References to “the Act” are references to the Renting Homes (Wales) Act 2016, as amended. The Act also covers statutory instruments created from powers granted within the Act.

References to "the Dwelling/Property" include reference to any part or parts of the Dwelling/Property and the land surrounding it together with the garden, garage and parking space (if applicable).

References to “Contract”, "this Contract" or “the Contract” are to this Contract.

References in this Contract to “occupation date” is the date on which the Contract-Holder is entitled to move into the Dwelling/Property. 

References to the "Fixtures and Fittings" means all items contained in the Inventory and signed on behalf of the parties at the commencement of the Contract or any items replacing them, including reference to any of the fixtures, fittings, furnishings or effects, floor, ceiling and wall coverings.

References to "the Term" or "the Contract" include any extension or continuation of the Contract or any periodic contract which may arise following the expiry or determination of the period of the Term specified in Clause 2\.

References in this Contract to "water charges" include references to sewerage and environmental service charges.

**References in this Contract to “service installation” means an installation for the supply of water, gas or electricity, for sanitation, for space heating or for heating water (F+).**

“the Deposit” means any single amount of money paid by the Contract-Holder or a third party to the Landlord or to the Agent under the Contract as security against the performance of the Contract-Holder’s obligations under the Contract, the discharge of any liabilities including those monies properly due and payable by the Contract-Holder to the Agent, any damage to the Dwelling/Property and/or non-payment of rent during the Contract. 

**The Landlord may not require security (which includes a deposit) to be given in any form other than — a) money, or b) a guarantee (F+).**

“The Deposit Holder” as mentioned in the Required Information pages shown at Annex 4 is the person, firm, or company who holds the Deposit under, and is a member of one of the organisations authorised to register Deposits under the Act.

Where the Deposit is held as “Stakeholder” no deductions can be made from the Deposit without consent, preferably in writing, from both parties, or from the court, or an adjudication decision from chosen deposit scheme.

“Relevant Persons” mentioned in the Required Information pages attached to this Contract means any other person or company paying the Deposit on behalf of the Contract-Holder, e.g. a local authority, parent, or Guarantor. Relevant persons will be given details of the scheme with which the deposit will be registered.

The expression "Landlord" shall include anyone lawfully entitled to the Dwelling/Property upon the termination of the Contract.

"The Landlord’s Agent" or “Agent” means the Landlord’s chosen agent, if applicable. 

"The Check-In Inventory and Schedule of Condition", “Inventory” means the document drawn up prior to the commencement of the Contract by the Landlord, the Landlord’s Agent or an inventory clerk.

"The Contract-Holder" includes anyone to whom the Contract has been lawfully transferred, including succession.

“Permitted Occupier", if used in the Contract, includes any person who is licensed by the Landlord to reside at the Dwelling/Property and who will be bound by all the terms of this Contract apart from the payment of rent.

Where the Contract-Holder is more than one person, the Contract-Holder's covenants are joint and several. The expression "joint and several" means that jointly the Contract-Holders are responsible for the payment of all rent and all liabilities falling upon the Contract-Holders during the Contract or any extension of it. Joint Contract-Holders have rights under the Act to sever their liability in certain circumstances. Individually, each Contract-Holder is also responsible for payment of all rent and all liabilities falling upon the Contract-Holders, as well as any breach of the Contract.

Any reference to any Act of Parliament, including devolved legislation passed through the Welsh Parliament, includes a reference to amended or replacement legislation and to subordinate legislation made under such Acts of Parliament.

“MyDeposits” means The Dispute Service as detailed in Clause 10 of this Contract.

“ICE” is an “Independent Case Examiner” of The Dispute Service Limited.

Any reference to the singular includes a reference to the plural and vice versa.
