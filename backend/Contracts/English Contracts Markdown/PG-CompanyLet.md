**Check-In Inventory and Schedule of Condition:** The 'Check-In Inventory and Schedule of Condition' or 'Inventory', if applicable, means the document drawn up prior to the commencement of the Tenancy by the Landlord, the Agent, or an inventory clerk and provided to the Tenant by the Landlord or the Agent. **Rent Payment Dates:** For the duration of the Tenancy, \[RENT DUE DATE\] of each month. Where the payment date falls on a non-banking day, the Tenant must ensure the payment is received by the Landlord by the next banking day.

**6\. Tenant's Obligations**

**6.1** The Tenant agrees and undertakes:

(a) to pay the rent on the due date and in the manner specified in this Agreement;

(b) to pay all charges for gas, electricity, water, sewerage, telephone, broadband, television licence and council tax (or such of them as are not included in the rent) attributable to the Property during the Tenancy;

(c) to keep the interior of the Property and the Fixtures and Fittings clean, tidy and in good condition and to leave them in the same condition (fair wear and tear excepted) at the end of this Agreement;

(d) not to cause or permit any nuisance, annoyance, disturbance, or inconvenience to the Landlord, any Agent, or to any neighbours or occupiers of adjoining properties;

(e) not to use the Property or allow it to be used for any illegal or immoral purpose;

(f) not to keep any pets at the Property without the prior written consent of the Landlord;

(g) not to smoke inside the Property unless the Landlord has given prior written consent;

(h) not to make any alterations or additions to the Property without the prior written consent of the Landlord;

(i) not to assign, sublet, or part with possession of the Property or any part thereof without the prior written consent of the Landlord;

(j) not to display any notice, sign, or advertisement at the Property;

(k) to allow the Landlord or Agent reasonable access to the Property for the purposes of inspection, repair, or maintenance, at reasonable times and with not less than 24 hours' written notice (except in the case of emergency);

(l) to report promptly to the Landlord or Agent any damage to the Property or any disrepair or defect in the Property of which the Tenant becomes aware;

(m) not to change the locks on any doors or windows at the Property without the prior written consent of the Landlord and to provide copies of all new keys;

(n) to provide the Landlord or Agent with a forwarding address at the end of this Agreement for the purposes of returning the Deposit and any correspondence;

(o) not to allow any person other than the Permitted Occupiers to reside at the Property without the prior written consent of the Landlord;

(p) to observe and comply with all laws, regulations, and requirements affecting the Property;

(q) to ensure that the Permitted Occupiers comply with the terms of this Agreement insofar as they relate to the occupation and use of the Property;

(r) to insure or be responsible for all personal belongings of the Permitted Occupiers at the Property, the Landlord accepting no liability for any loss or damage to the same;

(s) not to do or permit to be done anything which may vitiate or prejudice any insurance policy held by the Landlord in respect of the Property or which may increase the premium payable;

(t) to indemnify the Landlord against all losses, claims, demands, actions, proceedings, damages, costs or expenses or other liability arising in any way from the Tenant's or Permitted Occupiers' use of the Property or from any breach of the Tenant's obligations under this Agreement.

---

**7\. Landlord's Obligations**

**7.1** The Landlord agrees:

(a) to allow the Tenant to occupy the Property peacefully without unnecessary interruption or interference, provided the Tenant complies with the terms of this Agreement;

(b) to keep the structure and exterior of the Property in good repair, including drains, gutters and external pipes;

(c) to keep in repair and proper working order the installations in the Property for the supply of water, gas, electricity, sanitation, and for space and water heating;

(d) to ensure that the Property is fit for human habitation at the commencement of the Tenancy and throughout its duration;

(e) to insure the Property (but not the Tenant's or Permitted Occupiers' personal belongings) against the Insured Risks;

(f) to provide the Tenant with a valid Energy Performance Certificate for the Property;

(g) to comply with all relevant gas safety and electrical safety regulations;

(h) to ensure that any furniture and furnishings supplied comply with the Furniture and Furnishings (Fire) (Safety) Regulations 1988 (as amended);

(i) to return the Deposit in accordance with the provisions of this Agreement.

---

**8\. Landlord's Right of Entry**

**8.1** The Landlord or Agent may enter the Property at reasonable times on giving the Tenant not less than 24 hours' written notice for the purpose of:

(a) inspecting the condition and state of repair of the Property;

(b) carrying out repairs or maintenance to the Property;

(c) complying with any statutory obligation;

(d) showing prospective tenants or purchasers around the Property during the last two months of the Tenancy (or at any time after notice to terminate has been served).

**8.2** In the case of emergency, the Landlord or Agent may enter the Property without notice.

**10\. Termination**

\[TERMINATION\_SECTION\]

**10.1** Upon termination of this Agreement, the Tenant shall:

(a) deliver up vacant possession of the Property to the Landlord or Agent in the same condition as at the commencement of this Agreement (fair wear and tear excepted);

(b) remove all personal belongings of the Permitted Occupiers from the Property;

(c) return all keys, fobs, and security devices to the Landlord or Agent;

(d) provide the Landlord or Agent with a forwarding address.

**10.2** Any belongings left at the Property after 14 days following the termination date may be disposed of by the Landlord and the Tenant shall be liable for any reasonable costs of storage and disposal.

**10.3** Termination of this Agreement shall not affect any rights or obligations which have accrued prior to termination.

**11\. Forfeiture**

**11.1** The Landlord may re-enter the Property and this Agreement shall thereupon be determined (but without prejudice to any claim by the Landlord in respect of any antecedent breach of the Tenant's obligations) if:

(a) any rent is in arrears for more than \[FORFEITURE\_DAYS\] days after the date on which it became due (whether formally demanded or not);

(b) the Tenant is in breach of any of its obligations under this Agreement;

(c) the Tenant (being a company) enters into liquidation, whether compulsory or voluntary (save for the purposes of amalgamation or reconstruction), or has a receiver, administrator, or administrative receiver appointed, or suffers any analogous proceedings under the law of any other jurisdiction;

(d) the Tenant (being a company) makes any arrangement or composition with its creditors;

(e) any distress or execution is levied on the Property.

**11.2** Re-entry or forfeiture shall not affect the Landlord's right to claim damages or any other remedy to which the Landlord may be entitled.
